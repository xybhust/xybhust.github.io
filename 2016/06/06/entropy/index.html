Entropy and mutual information

Notes from PRML.

Let $H(x)$ denote the entropy of the random variable $x$, which measures the uncertainty of $x$.

Discrete Variables

$$ H(x) = -\sum_{i}p(x_i)\ln{p(x_i)} $$

Maximizing $H(x)$ subject to $\sum_{i}p(x_i)=1$ using Lagrange mulplier gives
$$ p(x_i) = \frac{1}{M}, $$
where $M$ is the number of possible values.

Continuous Variables

Letâ€™s specify a continuous random variable by preserving the first and second moments of the variable, which is $\mu$ and $\sigma$. Namely,
$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}p(x)dx = 1 $$
$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}xp(x)dx = \mu $$
$$ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}(x - \mu)^2p(x)dx = \sigma^2. $$

Then,
$$ H(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}p(x)\ln{p(x)}dx. $$
Maximazing $H(x)$ subject to the previous three constraints using Lagrange mulplier gives
$$ p(x) = \frac{1}{\left(2\pi\sigma^2 \right )^{1/2}}\exp\left\{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2} \right \}, $$
which implies the entropy is maximized when the random variable is Gaussian.

Conditional Entropy

$$ H(y|x) = \int\int p(y, x)\ln{p(y|x)dydx} $$

Using the fact that $p(y|x) = \frac{p(y, x)}{p(x)}$ gives the following relation
$$ H(x, y) = H(y|x) + H(x). $$

Relative Entropy and Mutual Information

Consider some unkown distribution $p(x)$, and suppose that we have modelled this using an approximating distribution $q(x)$.
\begin{align}
KL(p||q) &= -\int p(x)\ln{q(x)}dx - \left(-\int p(x)\ln{p(x)}dx \right ) \\
&= -\int p(x)\ln\left\{\frac{q(x)}{p(x)} \right \}dx
\end{align}

This is known as relative entropy or Kullback-Leibler divergence.

\begin{align}
I(x, y) &= KL\left(p(x,y)||p(x)p(y) \right ) \\
&= -\int\int p(x, y)\ln\left\{\frac{p(x)p(y)}{p(x, y)} \right \}dxdy
\end{align}

Using the fact that
$$\frac{p(x)p(y)}{p(x, y)} = \frac{p(y)}{p(y|x)} = \frac{p(x)}{p(x|y)}, $$
we have,
$$ I(x,y) = H(x) - H(x|y) = H(y) - H(y|x), $$

which is called the mutual information between variables $x$ and $y$.
