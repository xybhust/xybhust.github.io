Spectral decomposition, Cholesky decomposition

No proof, only conclusions.

Spectral decomposition is the factorization of a matrix into a canonical form, whereby the matrix is represented in terms of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors. Only diagonalizable matrices can be factorized in this way. Symmetric matrices must be diagonalizable matrices.
$$ \boldsymbol{A} = \sum_{i=1}^n{\lambda_i\boldsymbol{e}_i\boldsymbol{e}_i^T} $$
or
$$ \boldsymbol{A} = \boldsymbol{P\Lambda P}^T $$
where $\boldsymbol{\Lambda}$ is a diagonal matrix consisting of all eigenvalues of $\boldsymbol{A}$ and $\boldsymbol{P}$ is an $n\times n$ matrix consisting of the $n$ eigenvectors as columns.

Cholesky decomposition
For a symmetric matrix $\boldsymbol{A}$, there exists a lower triangular matrix $\boldsymbol{L}$ with diagonal elements being 1 and a diagonal matrix $\boldsymbol{G}$ such that $\boldsymbol{A} = \boldsymbol{LGL}^T$.

If $\boldsymbol{A}$ is positive definite, then the diagonal elements of $\boldsymbol{G}$ are positive. In this case, we have
$$ \boldsymbol{A} = \boldsymbol{L}\sqrt{\boldsymbol{G}}\sqrt{\boldsymbol{G}}\boldsymbol{L}^T = (\boldsymbol{L}\sqrt{\boldsymbol{G}})(\boldsymbol{L}\sqrt{\boldsymbol{G}})^T $$
where $\boldsymbol{L}\sqrt{\boldsymbol{G}}$ is again a lower triangular matrix and the square root is taken element by element.
